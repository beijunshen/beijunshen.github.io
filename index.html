Beijun Shen

Postdoctoral Researcher
Department of Mechanical Engineering
Columbia University

I am a postdoctoral researcher in mechanical engineering working on the mechanics and design of soft material systems. My research integrates experimental characterization, constitutive modeling, and computational mechanics to understand and design materials with programmable large-deformation and rate-dependent behavior.

My work focuses on architected soft materials, liquid crystal elastomers, and multimaterial polymer systems. I am particularly interested in how material composition, structural architecture, and spatial gradients in properties can be leveraged to design soft structures with enhanced mechanical performance and new functionalities.

Research Vision

My long-term research goal is to establish predictive mechanics frameworks for soft and multifunctional material systems. By integrating material manufacturing, experimental characterization, constitutive theory, and computational implementation, I aim to develop mechanics-guided strategies for designing soft materials with tailored mechanical response.

I am particularly interested in understanding how material composition, microstructure, and structural architecture interact across scales, and how gradients in material properties can be exploited to create soft systems with improved energy dissipation, mechanical robustness, and programmable functionality.

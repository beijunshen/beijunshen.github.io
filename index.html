Beijun Shen

Postdoctoral Researcher
Department of Mechanical Engineering
Columbia University, New York, NY, USA

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Columbia University. My research focuses on the mechanics and design of soft material systems, with an emphasis on architected materials, liquid crystal elastomers, multimaterial polymers, and graded soft structures. By integrating experimental characterization, constitutive modeling, and computational mechanics, I seek to establish predictive frameworks for large-deformation and rate-dependent material behavior.

More broadly, I am interested in mechanics-guided design of material systems that are adaptive, multifunctional, and manufacturable. My work is motivated by problems in soft robotic and biomedical systems, bio-inspired structures, and sustainable polymer materials, where the interplay among material composition, microstructure, and structural architecture governs performance and functionality.

My current research directions include:

  • Architected soft materials for programmable mechanical response and energy dissipation
  • Liquid crystal elastomers that couple viscoelasticity, soft elasticity, and structural instability
  • Multimaterial and graded polymer systems with composition-dependent constitutive behavior
  • Constitutive modeling and finite-element implementation for soft and multifunctional materials
  • Mechanics of recycled and heterogeneous polymer systems relevant to sustainability

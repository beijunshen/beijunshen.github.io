Beijun Shen

Postdoctoral Researcher
Department of Mechanical Engineering
Columbia University

I am a postdoctoral researcher in mechanical engineering working on the mechanics and design of soft material systems. My research integrates experiments, constitutive modeling, and computational mechanics to establish predictive frameworks for large-deformation and rate-dependent behavior in soft and multifunctional materials.

My work spans architected soft materials, liquid crystal elastomers, and multimaterial polymer systems. More broadly, I am interested in how material composition, structural architecture, and spatial gradients in properties can be used to design materials with tailored mechanical performance, energy dissipation, and functionality. These ideas are relevant to soft robotic and biomedical systems, biologically inspired structures, and sustainable polymer materials.

Research Overview

My long-term goal is to develop predictive mechanics frameworks for soft and multifunctional material systems by connecting material processing and manufacturing, experimental characterization, constitutive theory, and computational implementation. I am particularly interested in mechanics-guided design across scales, from polymer composition and microstructure to structural architecture and graded material distributions.

In addition to soft functional materials, I am also interested in the mechanics of sustainable polymer systems, including recycled and compositionally heterogeneous materials. More broadly, I aim to develop mechanics-based design principles for material systems that are high-performing, adaptive, and relevant to challenges in bio-inspired design, biomedical materials, and sustainability.

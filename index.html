Beijun Shen

Postdoctoral Researcher
Department of Mechanical Engineering
Columbia University, New York, NY, USA

My research focuses on the mechanics and design of soft and architected material systems. By integrating experiments, constitutive modeling, and computational mechanics, I study how material physics, structural architecture, and spatial material gradients interact to produce complex behaviors such as nonlinear elasticity, viscoelasticity, structural instability, and energy dissipation.

A central goal of my work is to establish predictive mechanics frameworks for multimaterial and graded polymer systems. My research combines advanced manufacturing, mechanical characterization, and finite-element implementation to investigate liquid crystal elastomers, digitally mixed multimaterial polymers, architected soft composites, and recycled polymer systems whose response spans the spectrum from elastomeric to glassy regimes. These systems provide a platform for mechanics-guided design of materials with programmable mechanical performance.

Inspired by natural systems such as mussel byssal threads, I also investigate bio-inspired graded materials in which spatial variations in composition and structure enable improved load transfer, energy dissipation, and resilience under dynamic loading. More broadly, I am interested in integrating mechanics-based modeling with physics-informed data-driven methods, including neural-network-assisted constitutive modeling and structural-scale simulations, to enable predictive and inverse design of complex material systems. These approaches connect material-point constitutive behavior with structural mechanics and digital-twin frameworks for programmable materials and engineered structures.

My long-term vision is to develop mechanics-guided design principles for adaptive, multifunctional, and sustainable material systems. This includes architected and graded soft materials enabled by multimaterial additive manufacturing as well as mechanics-informed strategies for circular polymer systems and high-value reuse of recycled plastics.

Current research directions include:

  • Architected soft materials for programmable mechanical response and energy dissipation
  • Liquid crystal elastomers coupling viscoelasticity, soft elasticity, and structural instability
  • Multimaterial and graded polymers with composition-dependent large-deformation behavior
  • Constitutive modeling and finite-element frameworks for rate-dependent soft materials
  • Physics-informed machine learning for constitutive modeling and digital-twin mechanics
  • Mechanics of recycled and heterogeneous polymer systems for sustainable materials

Beijun Shen

Postdoctoral Researcher
Department of Mechanical Engineering
Columbia University, New York, NY, USA

My research focuses on the mechanics and design of soft and architected material systems. By integrating experiments, constitutive modeling, and computational mechanics, I study how material composition, microstructure, and structural architecture interact to produce complex behaviors such as nonlinear elasticity, viscoelasticity, structural instability, and energy dissipation. My work investigates systems including liquid crystal elastomers, digitally mixed multimaterial polymers, graded soft materials, and recycled polymer systems, with the goal of establishing predictive frameworks for large-deformation and rate-dependent behavior.

More broadly, I aim to develop mechanics-guided design principles for programmable and sustainable material systems. My research explores bio-inspired material gradients, multimaterial additive manufacturing, and architected soft composites, while integrating physics-informed data-driven approaches such as neural-network-assisted constitutive modeling and structural simulations. These efforts connect material-point behavior with structural mechanics and enable predictive and inverse design frameworks for adaptive materials, digital twins, and circular polymer systems.

Current research directions include:

  • Architected soft materials for programmable mechanical response and energy dissipation
  • Liquid crystal elastomers coupling viscoelasticity, soft elasticity, and structural instability
  • Multimaterial and graded polymers with composition-dependent large-deformation behavior
  • Constitutive modeling and finite-element frameworks for rate-dependent soft materials
  • Physics-informed machine learning for constitutive modeling and digital-twin mechanics
  • Mechanics of recycled and heterogeneous polymer systems for sustainable materials

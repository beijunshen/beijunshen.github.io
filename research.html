Research

My research focuses on the mechanics and design of soft material systems across multiple length scales, with an emphasis on large deformation, rate dependence, instability, architecture, and material gradients.

1. Architected soft materials

I study how material constitutive behavior interacts with structural geometry to control stiffness, instability, energy dissipation, and recoverability in soft architected systems.

2. Liquid crystal elastomers

My work on liquid crystal elastomer systems explores how viscoelastic response, soft elasticity, and structural mechanisms such as snap-through can be coupled to achieve enhanced energy absorption and programmable mechanical behavior.

3. Multimaterial polymer systems

I investigate multimaterial and composition-dependent polymer systems, including digitally mixed soft materials and heterogeneous structures, with the goal of building predictive constitutive frameworks that span elastomeric to glassy response.

4. Graded soft materials and bio-inspired design

I am interested in spatially graded soft structures inspired by biological systems, where gradients in material properties and geometry can be used to control load transfer, energy dissipation, and damage resistance.

5. Constitutive modeling and computational mechanics

A central component of my research is constitutive modeling and computational implementation, including finite-strain viscoelastic and viscoplastic formulations, Abaqus UMAT development, and simulation-guided design of soft material systems.
